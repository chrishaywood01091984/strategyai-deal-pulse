01Sector heat

Deal velocity by sector across the six-month window — the most recent three months against the prior three. This is the rotation a partner pays Bloomberg for: which sectors are accelerating into mandate territory, and which are cooling.

Accelerating Rising Flat Cooling Bar = 6-month volume · figure = last 3 months vs prior 3 months
The StrategyAI read

Momentum is rotating, not rising across the board. Materials & Chemicals (+142%), Real Estate (+108%) and the financial sector (+92%) have sharply lifted their deal cadence into the most recent quarter, while Consumer & Retail has cooled by roughly a fifth. One caveat worth pricing in: the Real Estate surge is led by single-asset property trades that generate little operating-advisory work, whereas the FIG and Materials acceleration carries genuine mandate weight. The integration and synergy mandates that follow a deal wave land two-to-five months later, so the sectors accelerating now on quality, not just count are where pipeline should be built for the back half of the year — while Consumer teams defend incumbent relationships rather than chase a slowing market.

02Deal flow & capital

Monthly deal count against disclosed transaction value. Volume has climbed from 69 deals in November to 204 in May, with a $176bn capital peak in April — the busiest mandate-forming window of the half-year.

The StrategyAI read

Volume and capital move together, but they are not the same signal. Deal count roughly tripled from November's trough to 204 in May, while the $176bn April capital peak came from a handful of mega-deals rather than broad-based activity. Partners should read them separately: rising count means more mid-market integration work — the bread-and-butter of advisory — while the capital spikes flag the rare transformational deals where a single mandate can anchor a year's revenue.

03Sector momentum tape

Six-month trajectory for every tracked sector at a glance. Materials, Real Estate and FIG are accelerating hardest into the recent quarter; TMT remains the deepest market by volume.

The StrategyAI read

The tape separates structural depth from directional heat. TMT and FIG are the deepest markets by raw volume and the safest places to keep a standing sector team. The steepest recent slopes are Materials, Real Estate and FIG — but Real Estate's is inflated by a cluster of single-asset property trades that throw off little integration work, so on mandate quality the cleaner accelerations to lean into are FIG, Materials and Business Services. Depth tells a partner where to be permanently; slope tells them where to lean in this quarter.

The StrategyAI read

Volume and conviction point in different directions, and the gap is the opportunity. Post-acquisition integration is 79% of all flow but scores a middling 6.1/10 — reliable, competitive, commoditised work. The play is a barbell: industrialise the PMI volume to stay in the flow, and hunt selectively for the high-conviction patterns you can win at premium rates.
